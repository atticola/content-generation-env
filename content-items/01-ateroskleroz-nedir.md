### Content Long (English)

**What is atherosclerosis?**

Atherosclerosis is the hardening and narrowing of arteries caused by the buildup of plaqueâ€”composed of fats, cholesterol, and other substancesâ€”on the inner walls of arteries. This condition can restrict blood flow, preventing adequate oxygen and nutrients from reaching the heart, brain, and other organs.

**How does it develop?**

Atherosclerosis is a slowly progressive process:
- LDL cholesterol begins accumulating in artery walls
- Inflammatory response develops over time
- Plaque grows and hardens
- Arteries lose elasticity
- Blood flow slows or may stop

**Risk factors:**
- High LDL cholesterol
- High blood pressure
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Family history

**What are the consequences?**

Atherosclerosis can lead to serious complications such as heart attack, stroke, and peripheral artery disease. However, with early detection and appropriate measures, these risks can be reduced.

**Preventive measures:**
- Healthy, balanced diet
- Regular physical activity
- Avoid smoking
- Regular health check-ups
- Control of risk factors
