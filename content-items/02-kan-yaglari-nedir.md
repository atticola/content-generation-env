### Content Long (English)

**What are blood lipids?**

Blood lipids (lipids) are fat-like substances in the bloodstream that play important roles in energy storage, cell structure, and hormone production. Maintaining healthy levels is critical for heart and vascular health.

**Types of blood lipids:**

1. **LDL Cholesterol (Bad Cholesterol)**
   - Can accumulate in artery walls causing plaque formation
   - High levels increase heart disease risk
   - Primary lipid target for treatment

2. **HDL Cholesterol (Good Cholesterol)**
   - Helps remove excess cholesterol from arteries
   - Has protective effects
   - Higher HDL is generally beneficial

3. **Triglycerides**
   - Form of energy storage in the body
   - High levels increase cardiovascular risk
   - Especially important in diabetes and metabolic syndrome

4. **Non-HDL Cholesterol**
   - Total cholesterol minus HDL
   - Additional risk indicator beyond LDL

**Why is it important?**

Knowing your blood lipid levels helps assess your risk of heart attack and stroke. Regular lipid panel testing is the first step in identifying your risk factors.
